1

Culture / Western Coast

A Maronite Village at the Edge of the Island

Koruçam preserves a distinctive rhythm shaped by Maronite heritage, village streets and the landscapes of the western coast. The route combines cultural details with quiet roads leading toward the cape.

Recommendation: Visit village bakeries early in the day, as traditional bread and pastries may sell out before the afternoon.

View Route ↗

Lapta’s long coastal promenade during the cooler evening light.

2

Village / Coast

Where the Mountains Meet the Coastal Walk

Lapta brings together the historic upper village, traditional water sources and a long promenade facing the sea. It works especially well as a slow afternoon route.

Recommendation: Begin the coastal walk during the hour before sunset, when the promenade is cooler and the mountains catch the last light.

View Route ↗

Buffavento’s stone walls climbing the rugged Kyrenia ridge.

3

Mountains / History

Stone Walls Above the Northern Coast

Buffavento rises high above the Kyrenia range, combining steep mountain scenery with the remains of one of the island’s most dramatic castles. The journey is as important as the viewpoint itself.

Recommendation: Start early and carry water, because cloud and wind conditions can change quickly near the castle ridge.

View Route ↗

Kantara Castle standing above the eastern Kyrenia range.

4

Castle / Panorama

A View Across the Eastern Landscape

Kantara offers a wide perspective across the eastern mountains, coast and inland plains. Its position makes the castle one of the strongest panoramic stops on the island.

Recommendation: The clearest long-distance views often appear after windy or rainy weather has cleared the air.

View Route ↗

The open coast near Bafra on the approach to the Karpas peninsula.

5

Vineyards / Coast

From Village Roads to the Karpas Sea

This eastern route connects vineyard country, open coastal landscapes and the working harbour at Boğaz. It creates a gradual transition from village life toward the Karpas coastline.

Recommendation: Combine the village and vineyard area with a late lunch near Boğaz harbour instead of rushing directly toward Bafra.
