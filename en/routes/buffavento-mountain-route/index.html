Kyrenia Range Mountain Route

Buffavento Mountain & Forgotten Monasteries

Follow the Kyrenia mountain range through a dramatic medieval castle, forest landscapes, remote religious sites and panoramic viewpoints above the northern coast.

Route overview

High mountain roads and hidden heritage

This route crosses the central Kyrenia mountain range and connects some of Northern Cyprus’s most dramatic historic locations. It begins with the climb to Buffavento Castle, continues through the forests around Alevkayası and then visits two remote religious sites before finishing at a mountain viewpoint above Esentepe.

Five selected stops

Route itinerary

1

Buffavento Castle

Begin at one of the highest and most dramatic castles in Cyprus. The final approach requires a steep uphill walk, but the ruins and views across the Kyrenia range make it the defining stop of this mountain route.

2

Alevkayası Picnic Area

Continue toward the forested landscape around Alevkayası. The picnic area offers a quieter place to rest after the castle climb and provides a different view of the mountain environment.

3

Sourp Magar Monastery

Visit the remains of this historic Armenian monastery within the mountain landscape. The site is remote and atmospheric, with ruined buildings surrounded by forest and open views toward the northern coast.

4

Antiphonitis Church

Continue east toward Antiphonitis, a historic church situated among the mountain foothills. Its architecture and surviving religious artwork make it one of the route’s most important cultural stops.

5

Esentepe Mountain Viewpoint

Finish at a viewpoint above Esentepe, where the landscape opens across the northern coastline and the eastern Kyrenia mountains. It is a suitable final stop for late-afternoon light and panoramic photography.

Before you drive

Practical information

Best time

Begin early in the morning so the Buffavento climb is completed before the strongest midday heat.

Road conditions

Expect narrow, winding and uneven mountain roads. Drive slowly and avoid continuing in poor weather or after dark.

What to bring

  • Strong walking shoes
  • Plenty of drinking water
  • Sun and wind protection
  • Food for the full route

Castle climb

The walk to Buffavento Castle is steep and exposed. Allow enough time for breaks and do not attempt it in extreme heat.

Heritage sites

Historic buildings may have uneven floors, loose stone and restricted areas. Stay on safe paths and do not climb damaged structures.

Fuel and signal

Begin with sufficient fuel and download maps in advance, as mobile coverage can be unreliable in parts of the mountain range.

Recommendation

Complete the Buffavento Castle climb first and use Alevkayası as the main rest stop afterwards. Carry your own food and water because services between the mountain locations are limited and opening times near the religious sites may vary.
