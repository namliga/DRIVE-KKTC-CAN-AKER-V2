Kyrenia West Village & Coastal Route

Lapta Village & Coastal Walk

Connect the historic upper village, natural water sources and traditional channels with one of the longest coastal walking routes west of Kyrenia.

Stops 5 locations
Duration Half day
Difficulty Easy
Route type Village & coast

Route overview

From the mountain village to the Mediterranean coast

This route begins among the historic streets of upper Lapta before following the village’s relationship with natural springs and traditional water systems. It then descends toward the coast, where the route continues along the Lapta Coastal Walkway and finishes with open sunset views near Karşıyaka.

Five selected stops

Route itinerary

1

Lapta Old Village

Begin in the upper part of Lapta, where narrow streets, stone buildings, village homes and mountain views preserve the character of the older settlement. Walk through the centre before continuing toward the natural springs.

2

Başpınar Spring

Visit one of the water sources associated with Lapta’s historical development. The spring reflects the importance of mountain water for village life, agriculture and the settlements that developed along the northern slopes.

3

Historic Lapta Water Channels

Follow sections of the old water system that once carried spring water through the village and surrounding agricultural areas. Some sections are subtle, so explore slowly and avoid entering private property.

4

Lapta Coastal Walkway

Continue toward the sea and join the long coastal promenade. Bridges and seaside sections create an easy walking route with views across the Mediterranean and back toward the Kyrenia mountains.

5

Karşıyaka Sunset Coast

Finish west of Lapta along the quieter Karşıyaka coastline. The open orientation and lower development make this area suitable for watching the sunset and ending the route beside the sea.

Before you drive

Practical information

Best time

Begin in the village during the afternoon and reach the coastal walkway around one hour before sunset.

Road conditions

Roads through the upper village can be narrow. Park without blocking homes, entrances or local traffic.

What to bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Drinking water
  • Sun protection
  • A light layer for the evening

Walking distance

The full coastal promenade can take longer than expected. Select a shorter section when daylight or time is limited.

Village access

Some historic streets are steep or uneven. Walk carefully and respect residential areas.

Coastal conditions

The walkway is exposed to wind and sea spray. Avoid slippery sections during rough weather.

Recommendation

Do not begin with the coastal walkway. Start in upper Lapta while the village is still active, then descend toward the sea and finish the route around sunset when the promenade is cooler and the mountains catch the final light.
